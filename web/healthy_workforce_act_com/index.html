Wellness Tax Credit

Wellness Program Information
A current U.S. Senate bill, sponsored by Earl Blumenauer, will give significant tax breaks to employers initiating health and wellness programs in the workplace. The Healthy Workforce Act will grant tax credits of $200 per employee at businesses with 200 employees or less enrolled in wellness programs. Businesses with 200 employees or more will receive tax credits of $100 per employee.

Prompted by Congress research that finds "chronic diseases such as heart disease, stroke, cancer, obesity, and diabetes are among the most prevalent and costly worker health problems for most employers," the government is offering employers these incentives which will last up to 10 taxable years after the program implementation.

For a wellness program to qualify it must contain four components:

  1. Health Awareness:
    • Health education and health screenings


  2. Employee Engagement:
    • Worksite wellness programs, assessments, evaluations and tracking of employee participation


  3. Behavioral Change:
    • Seminars, online programs or self-help materials
    • Some suggested programs are tobacco use, obesity, stress management, physical fitness, substance abuse, depression and mental health promotion


  4. Supportive Environment:
    • Incentives for participation in health screenings or behavioral health programs
